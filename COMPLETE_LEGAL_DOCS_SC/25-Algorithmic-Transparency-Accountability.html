HINGECRAFT GLOBAL, LLC

ALGORITHMIC TRANSPARENCY &
ACCOUNTABILITY POLICY

Effective Date: December 6, 2025
State of South Carolina

1. PURPOSE

This Algorithmic Transparency & Accountability Policy ("Policy") establishes HingeCraft Global, LLC's ("HingeCraft") commitment to transparency and accountability in the use of automated decision-making systems and algorithms.

2. SCOPE

This Policy applies to all algorithms and automated systems that: (a) Make or significantly influence decisions affecting individuals; (b) Process personal data; (c) Provide recommendations or predictions; (d) Automate previously human-performed tasks.

3. TRANSPARENCY PRINCIPLES

3.1 Notice

We provide notice when: (a) Algorithms are used in decision-making; (b) Automated systems process personal data; (c) Recommendations are algorithmically generated.

3.2 Explanation

We explain: (a) The general logic of significant algorithmic decisions; (b) Key factors influencing outcomes; (c) How to contest or seek review of decisions.

3.3 Documentation

We document: (a) Purpose and function of algorithms; (b) Data inputs and outputs; (c) Training data and methodology; (d) Testing and validation results; (e) Known limitations.

4. ACCOUNTABILITY FRAMEWORK

4.1 Ownership

Each algorithm has a designated owner responsible for: (a) Compliance with this Policy; (b) Accuracy and performance; (c) Ongoing monitoring; (d) Responding to concerns.

4.2 Review

Algorithms undergo: (a) Pre-deployment review; (b) Regular performance audits; (c) Bias assessments; (d) Impact assessments for significant changes.

4.3 Human Oversight

Significant algorithmic decisions are subject to: (a) Human review capability; (b) Override mechanisms; (c) Appeal processes.

5. RIGHTS OF INDIVIDUALS

Individuals affected by algorithmic decisions have the right to: (a) Know when algorithms are used; (b) Understand the basis for decisions; (c) Request human review; (d) Contest algorithmic decisions; (e) Opt out where legally permitted.

6. GOVERNANCE

The AI Ethics Committee oversees algorithmic accountability. Annual reports are provided to leadership on algorithmic performance and issues.
