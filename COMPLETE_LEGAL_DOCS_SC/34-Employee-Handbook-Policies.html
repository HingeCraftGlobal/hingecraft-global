WELCOME

Welcome to HingeCraft Global, LLC. This Employee Handbook provides an overview of our policies, procedures, and expectations. It is not a contract of employment. Employment with HingeCraft is at-will, meaning either party may end the relationship at any time.

1. EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

1.1 At-Will Employment

Employment with HingeCraft is at-will. Either you or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice. This Handbook does not create a contract of employment.

1.2 Equal Employment Opportunity

HingeCraft is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy), national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic under federal, state, or local law.

1.3 Reasonable Accommodations

We provide reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with disabilities and for religious practices, unless doing so would impose an undue hardship.

1.4 Immigration Compliance

All employees must complete Form I-9 and provide documentation verifying identity and work authorization. HingeCraft participates in E-Verify.

2. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

2.1 Pay Practices

Employees are paid [bi-weekly / semi-monthly]. Pay is by direct deposit. Pay stubs are available online.

2.2 Work Hours

Standard work hours are [8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday]. Schedules may vary by position. Non-exempt employees must accurately record all time worked.

2.3 Overtime

Non-exempt employees receive overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Overtime must be pre-approved.

2.4 Benefits

Eligible employees may receive: (a) Health insurance; (b) Dental and vision insurance; (c) 401(k) retirement plan; (d) Paid time off; (e) Life insurance; (f) Disability insurance. Details are in benefit plan documents.

3. TIME OFF

3.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)

Full-time employees accrue PTO based on length of service. PTO must be requested in advance and approved by your manager.

3.2 Holidays

HingeCraft observes the following paid holidays: New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents Day, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving (2 days), Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, and New Year's Eve.

3.3 Leave of Absence

Leaves of absence may be available for: (a) Family and Medical Leave (FMLA); (b) Military service; (c) Jury duty; (d) Bereavement; (e) Personal leave (at Company discretion).

4. WORKPLACE CONDUCT

4.1 Standards of Conduct

Employees are expected to: (a) Perform duties competently; (b) Follow policies and procedures; (c) Treat others with respect; (d) Maintain confidentiality; (e) Represent the Company professionally; (f) Report concerns promptly.

4.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following may result in disciplinary action: (a) Harassment or discrimination; (b) Violence or threats; (c) Theft or dishonesty; (d) Substance abuse; (e) Insubordination; (f) Safety violations; (g) Conflict of interest; (h) Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information.

4.3 Harassment Prevention

HingeCraft prohibits harassment of any kind. Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature. Report harassment immediately to HR or use the ethics hotline.

4.4 Anti-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith is strictly prohibited.

5. HEALTH AND SAFETY

5.1 Safety Policy

Safety is a top priority. Employees must: (a) Follow all safety rules; (b) Use required protective equipment; (c) Report hazards immediately; (d) Report injuries promptly; (e) Participate in safety training.

5.2 Drug-Free Workplace

HingeCraft maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, sale, or distribution of illegal drugs or alcohol on Company premises is prohibited. Employees may be subject to drug testing.

5.3 Violence Prevention

Violence, threats, and intimidation are not tolerated. Report concerns immediately.

6. TECHNOLOGY AND COMMUNICATIONS

6.1 Acceptable Use

Company technology resources are for business use. Limited personal use is permitted. Do not use for illegal, harassing, or inappropriate purposes.

6.2 No Expectation of Privacy

The Company may monitor use of its technology resources. You have no expectation of privacy in Company systems.

6.3 Social Media

Personal social media use should not: (a) Disclose confidential information; (b) Represent personal views as Company positions; (c) Damage the Company's reputation; (d) Interfere with work.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY

Employees must protect confidential information including trade secrets, customer data, financial information, and proprietary technology. Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends.

8. CONFLICT OF INTEREST

Avoid situations where personal interests conflict with Company interests. Disclose potential conflicts to your manager or HR.

9. SEPARATION

9.1 Resignation

Employees who resign should provide at least two weeks' notice.

9.2 Termination

The Company may terminate employment at any time consistent with at-will employment.

9.3 Exit Process

Departing employees must: (a) Return all Company property; (b) Complete exit interview; (c) Transition work responsibilities.

10. ACKNOWLEDGMENT
