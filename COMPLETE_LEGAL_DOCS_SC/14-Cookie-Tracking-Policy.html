This Cookie & Tracking Policy ("Policy") explains how HingeCraft Global, LLC ("HingeCraft," "we," "us," or "our") uses cookies, web beacons, pixels, and similar tracking technologies on our website at https://www.hingecraft.com and related digital properties (collectively, the "Site").

2. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

2.1 Definition. Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) when you visit a website. Cookies allow the website to recognize your device and remember information about your visit.

2.2 Types of Cookies.

  • (a) First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you are visiting (hingecraft.com);
  • (b) Third-Party Cookies: Set by domains other than the website you are visiting;
  • (c) Session Cookies: Temporary cookies deleted when you close your browser;
  • (d) Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device until they expire or are deleted.

4. OTHER TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

4.1 Web Beacons (Pixels)

Web beacons are tiny transparent images embedded in web pages or emails that help us track:

  • (a) Whether a web page or email was viewed;
  • (b) When and how many times it was viewed;
  • (c) What actions were taken (clicks, conversions);
  • (d) Technical information (IP address, browser type).

4.2 Local Storage

We may use local storage (HTML5) to store data locally on your device. This is similar to cookies but can store more data.

4.3 Session Replay

We use session replay tools (e.g., Hotjar) to record user sessions for UX research. These recordings capture:

  • (a) Mouse movements and clicks;
  • (b) Scrolling behavior;
  • (c) Page interactions;
  • (d) Session duration.

Sensitive information (passwords, payment details) is masked in recordings.

When you first visit our Site, you will see a cookie consent banner that allows you to:

5.2 Cookie Settings

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by:

5.3 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through settings. You can:

  • (d) Be notified when a cookie is set.

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. We currently do not respond to DNT signals because there is no industry standard for how to respond. We will update this Policy if a standard is established.

We honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals. If your browser sends a GPC signal, we will treat it as a request to opt out of "sales" or "sharing" under applicable privacy laws.
