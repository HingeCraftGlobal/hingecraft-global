INTRODUCTION

This Cookie & Tracking Policy explains how HingeCraft Global ("we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and platform. This policy complies with GDPR, CCPA, and ePrivacy Directive requirements.

I. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and improve your experience.

II. TYPES OF COOKIES WE USE

2.1 Essential Cookies

Purpose: Required for website functionality

  • Authentication and login
  • Security features
  • Load balancing
  • Session management

Legal Basis: Legitimate interest (necessary for service provision)

Retention: Session or up to 1 year

2.2 Functional Cookies

Purpose: Enhance functionality and personalization

  • Remember preferences
  • Language settings
  • User interface customization
  • Chat functionality

Legal Basis: Consent (opt-in required)

Retention: Up to 2 years

2.3 Analytics Cookies

Purpose: Understand how visitors use our website

  • Page views and navigation
  • User behavior analysis
  • Performance metrics
  • Error tracking

Legal Basis: Consent (opt-in required)

Retention: Up to 2 years

Third Parties: Google Analytics (anonymized IP addresses)

2.4 Marketing Cookies

Purpose: Deliver relevant advertisements

  • Ad targeting
  • Campaign measurement
  • Retargeting
  • Social media integration

Legal Basis: Consent (opt-in required)

Retention: Up to 2 years

Third Parties: Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, LinkedIn Insight Tag

III. TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

3.1 Web Beacons

Small images embedded in emails and web pages to track engagement.

3.2 Pixel Tags

Used for tracking conversions and measuring ad effectiveness.

3.3 Local Storage

Browser storage used to remember preferences and improve performance.

IV. THIRD-PARTY COOKIES

We use third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Google Analytics: Website analytics
  • Stripe: Payment processing
  • Wix: Platform hosting
  • Social Media: Social sharing features

These third parties have their own privacy policies.

V. YOUR RIGHTS AND CHOICES

5.1 Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, you can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize cookie preferences

5.2 Managing Cookies

You can manage cookies through:

  • Cookie Settings: Access via cookie banner or footer link
  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies
  • Opt-Out Tools: Industry opt-out tools (e.g., DAA, NAI)

5.3 Browser Instructions

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Cookies
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy → Cookies

5.4 Impact of Disabling Cookies

Disabling cookies may affect:

  • Website functionality
  • Personalization features
  • User experience
  • Some features may not work

VI. DO NOT TRACK SIGNALS

We respect "Do Not Track" browser signals. When enabled, we limit tracking to essential cookies only.

VII. CHILDREN'S PRIVACY

We do not knowingly use tracking technologies to collect information from children under 13 without parental consent, in compliance with COPPA.

VIII. UPDATES TO THIS POLICY

We may update this Cookie Policy periodically. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.
