HINGECRAFT GLOBAL, LLC

CORPORATE SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY & COMPLIANCE POLICY

Effective Date: December 6, 2025
State of South Carolina

SECTION 1 - STATEMENT OF COMMITMENT

1.1 Commitment. HingeCraft Global, LLC (the "Company") is committed to conducting business in a socially responsible manner that benefits our stakeholders, communities, and the environment. We believe that long-term business success is built upon ethical practices, environmental stewardship, and positive contributions to society.

1.2 Core Principles. Our corporate social responsibility efforts are guided by the following core principles:

  • (a) Ethical Business Practices: Conducting all business with integrity, honesty, and transparency;
  • (b) Environmental Stewardship: Minimizing our environmental impact and promoting sustainability;
  • (c) Employee Well-being: Providing a safe, fair, and supportive workplace;
  • (d) Community Engagement: Contributing positively to the communities where we operate;
  • (e) Stakeholder Value: Creating sustainable value for all stakeholders;
  • (f) Human Rights: Respecting and supporting internationally recognized human rights.

SECTION 2 - ENVIRONMENTAL RESPONSIBILITY

2.1 Environmental Policy

The Company is committed to protecting the environment and minimizing the environmental impact of our operations. We will:

  • (a) Comply with all applicable environmental laws and regulations, including those administered by the South Carolina Department of Health and Environmental Control (SCDHEC) and the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA);
  • (b) Continuously improve our environmental performance through measurable objectives and targets;
  • (c) Prevent pollution and minimize waste at the source;
  • (d) Use natural resources efficiently and promote conservation;
  • (e) Design products with environmental considerations, including energy efficiency, recyclability, and reduced use of hazardous materials;
  • (f) Educate and train employees on environmental responsibilities;
  • (g) Communicate openly with stakeholders about our environmental performance.

2.2 Emissions and Energy

The Company commits to:

  • (a) Measuring and reporting greenhouse gas emissions;
  • (b) Setting and pursuing emission reduction targets;
  • (c) Improving energy efficiency in all operations;
  • (d) Investing in renewable energy where feasible;
  • (e) Reducing air emissions from manufacturing operations.

2.3 Waste and Materials

The Company commits to:

  • (a) Reducing waste generation through process optimization;
  • (b) Maximizing recycling and reuse of materials;
  • (c) Properly managing and disposing of hazardous materials;
  • (d) Working with suppliers to reduce packaging;
  • (e) Promoting circular economy principles.

2.4 Water Conservation

The Company commits to:

  • (a) Using water efficiently in all operations;
  • (b) Treating wastewater before discharge;
  • (c) Preventing pollution of water resources;
  • (d) Monitoring water usage and setting reduction targets.

SECTION 3 - SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY

3.1 Human Rights

The Company respects and supports internationally recognized human rights. We will:

  • (a) Not use or tolerate forced labor, child labor, or human trafficking in any form;
  • (b) Respect workers' rights to freedom of association and collective bargaining;
  • (c) Provide fair wages and reasonable working hours;
  • (d) Not discriminate in employment based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or other protected characteristics;
  • (e) Provide a safe and healthy workplace free from harassment;
  • (f) Conduct human rights due diligence in our supply chain;
  • (g) Provide access to remedy for adverse human rights impacts.

3.2 Labor Practices

The Company is committed to fair labor practices:

  • (a) Compliance with all applicable labor laws, including the Fair Labor Standards Act and South Carolina labor laws;
  • (b) Payment of at least minimum wage, with competitive compensation for our industry;
  • (c) Reasonable working hours with appropriate overtime compensation;
  • (d) Safe working conditions that comply with OSHA requirements;
  • (e) Equal employment opportunity and non-discrimination;
  • (f) Training and development opportunities for employees;
  • (g) Open communication and employee engagement.

3.3 Diversity and Inclusion

The Company values diversity and is committed to creating an inclusive workplace:

  • (a) Building a diverse workforce that reflects our communities;
  • (b) Providing equal opportunity in hiring, promotion, and compensation;
  • (c) Creating an inclusive culture where all employees feel valued;
  • (d) Providing diversity and inclusion training;
  • (e) Setting diversity goals and tracking progress;
  • (f) Partnering with diverse suppliers.

3.4 Health and Safety

The Company is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace:

  • (a) Compliance with all applicable health and safety laws and regulations;
  • (b) Identifying and eliminating workplace hazards;
  • (c) Providing appropriate personal protective equipment;
  • (d) Training employees on health and safety procedures;
  • (e) Investigating incidents and implementing corrective actions;
  • (f) Promoting employee wellness programs;
  • (g) Setting safety goals and tracking performance.

SECTION 4 - COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

4.1 Community Investment

The Company is committed to investing in the communities where we operate:

  • (a) Supporting local charitable organizations and community initiatives;
  • (b) Encouraging and supporting employee volunteerism;
  • (c) Providing educational opportunities and scholarships;
  • (d) Partnering with local organizations on community projects;
  • (e) Supporting local economic development.

4.2 Charitable Giving

The Company allocates resources for charitable giving, focused on:

  • (a) Education and workforce development;
  • (b) Environmental conservation;
  • (c) Community health and well-being;
  • (d) Arts and culture;
  • (e) Disaster relief and emergency response.

4.3 Employee Volunteerism

The Company encourages employees to volunteer in their communities:

  • (a) Providing paid volunteer time off;
  • (b) Organizing company volunteer events;
  • (c) Matching employee charitable contributions;
  • (d) Recognizing employee volunteer efforts.

SECTION 5 - ETHICAL BUSINESS PRACTICES

5.1 Anti-Corruption

The Company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for corruption and bribery:

  • (a) Prohibition of all forms of bribery and corruption;
  • (b) Compliance with the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act and applicable anti-corruption laws;
  • (c) Due diligence on business partners and intermediaries;
  • (d) Accurate books and records;
  • (e) Training on anti-corruption policies;
  • (f) Reporting mechanisms for suspected violations.

5.2 Fair Competition

The Company competes fairly and ethically:

  • (a) Compliance with antitrust and competition laws;
  • (b) No price fixing, bid rigging, or market allocation;
  • (c) No misappropriation of competitor information;
  • (d) Honest and accurate marketing and advertising.

5.3 Data Privacy and Security

The Company protects personal information:

  • (a) Compliance with applicable privacy laws;
  • (b) Collection of personal information only for legitimate purposes;
  • (c) Appropriate security measures to protect data;
  • (d) Transparency about data practices;
  • (e) Respect for individual privacy rights.

SECTION 6 - SUPPLY CHAIN RESPONSIBILITY

6.1 Supplier Code of Conduct

The Company expects its suppliers to uphold similar standards of social responsibility:

  • (a) Compliance with all applicable laws;
  • (b) Respect for human rights and prohibition of forced labor and child labor;
  • (c) Fair labor practices and safe working conditions;
  • (d) Environmental responsibility;
  • (e) Ethical business practices;
  • (f) Anti-corruption and anti-bribery compliance.

6.2 Supply Chain Due Diligence

The Company conducts due diligence on its supply chain:

  • (a) Supplier assessments and audits;
  • (b) Risk-based monitoring;
  • (c) Corrective action plans for non-compliance;
  • (d) Termination of relationships with suppliers who fail to meet standards.

6.3 Conflict Minerals

The Company is committed to responsible sourcing of minerals:

  • (a) Due diligence on the source of conflict minerals (tin, tantalum, tungsten, gold);
  • (b) Support for conflict-free supply chains;
  • (c) Compliance with Dodd-Frank Act Section 1502 if applicable;
  • (d) Transparency in reporting.

SECTION 7 - GOVERNANCE AND ACCOUNTABILITY

7.1 CSR Governance

Responsibility for CSR is assigned as follows:

  • (a) Board oversight of CSR strategy and performance;
  • (b) Senior management accountability for CSR implementation;
  • (c) CSR coordinator or committee to manage day-to-day activities;
  • (d) Integration of CSR into business operations.

7.2 Goals and Metrics

The Company sets measurable CSR goals and tracks progress:

  • (a) Environmental targets (emissions, waste, water, energy);
  • (b) Social targets (safety, diversity, community investment);
  • (c) Governance targets (ethics training, compliance);
  • (d) Regular reporting on progress.

7.3 Reporting and Transparency

The Company is committed to transparency:

  • (a) Annual CSR/sustainability reporting;
  • (b) Disclosure of relevant metrics and performance;
  • (c) Stakeholder engagement and feedback;
  • (d) Response to stakeholder inquiries.

7.4 Compliance

The Company maintains a robust compliance program:

  • (a) Code of conduct and ethics policies;
  • (b) Compliance training for all employees;
  • (c) Confidential reporting mechanisms (ethics hotline);
  • (d) Investigation and resolution of concerns;
  • (e) Non-retaliation policy for good-faith reporters;
  • (f) Regular compliance audits.

SECTION 8 - IMPLEMENTATION

8.1 Communication. This Policy will be communicated to all employees, suppliers, and stakeholders.

8.2 Training. Employees will receive training on CSR policies and expectations.

8.3 Review. This Policy will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary.

8.4 Questions. Questions about this Policy should be directed to the CSR coordinator or legal@hingecraft.com.
