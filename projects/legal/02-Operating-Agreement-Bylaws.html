HINGECRAFT GLOBAL, LLC

OPERATING AGREEMENT

A South Carolina Limited Liability Company
Effective Date: December 6, 2025

THIS OPERATING AGREEMENT (this "Agreement") of HingeCraft Global, LLC, a South Carolina limited liability company (the "Company"), is entered into and shall be effective as of the date set forth above, by and among the Company and the persons executing this Agreement as Members.

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company was formed as a limited liability company under the laws of the State of South Carolina by filing Articles of Organization with the South Carolina Secretary of State pursuant to the South Carolina Uniform Limited Liability Company Act of 1996 (S.C. Code Ann. § 33-44-101 et seq.) (the "Act"); and

WHEREAS, the Members desire to enter into this Operating Agreement to set forth the terms and conditions governing the operation and management of the Company, the rights and obligations of the Members, and other matters relating to the Company.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements hereinafter set forth and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE I - DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

1.1 "Act" means the South Carolina Uniform Limited Liability Company Act of 1996, S.C. Code Ann. § 33-44-101 et seq., as amended from time to time.

1.2 "Affiliate" means, with respect to any Person, any other Person that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with such Person.

1.3 "Agreement" means this Operating Agreement, as originally executed and as amended, modified, supplemented, or restated from time to time.

1.4 "Articles of Organization" means the Articles of Organization of the Company filed with the South Carolina Secretary of State, as amended from time to time.

1.5 "Capital Account" means the capital account maintained for each Member in accordance with Article V.

1.6 "Capital Contribution" means any contribution to the capital of the Company in cash, property, services rendered, or a promissory note or other obligation to contribute cash, property, or services.

1.7 "Code" means the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended from time to time.

1.8 "Company" means HingeCraft Global, LLC, a South Carolina limited liability company.

1.9 "Distributable Cash" means all cash received by the Company from Company operations or other sources (including proceeds from the sale of Company assets and borrowings), less (a) amounts used to pay or establish reserves for Company expenses, debt payments, capital improvements, and other amounts that the Members reasonably determine are necessary for the proper operation of the Company's business, and (b) amounts that the Members determine to retain as working capital.

1.10 "Distribution" means any transfer of money or other property by the Company to a Member on account of a Membership Interest.

1.11 "Economic Interest" means a Member's share of the Company's profits, losses, and Distributions.

1.12 "Fiscal Year" means the Company's fiscal year, which shall be the calendar year.

1.13 "Majority Vote" or "Majority in Interest" means the affirmative vote or consent of Members holding more than fifty percent (50%) of the total Percentage Interests of all Members entitled to vote.

1.14 "Manager" means any Person appointed to manage the business and affairs of the Company, if the Company is Manager-Managed.

1.15 "Member" means any Person who (a) has been admitted to the Company as a Member in accordance with this Agreement, (b) holds a Membership Interest in the Company, and (c) has not ceased to be a Member.

1.16 "Membership Interest" means all of a Member's rights in the Company, including the Member's Economic Interest, the Member's right to vote or participate in management, and the Member's right to information concerning the Company's business and affairs.

1.17 "Operating Agreement" means this Operating Agreement.

1.18 "Percentage Interest" means, with respect to any Member, the percentage set forth opposite such Member's name on Exhibit A, as amended from time to time.

1.19 "Person" means any individual, corporation, partnership, limited liability company, trust, estate, or other entity.

1.20 "Supermajority Vote" means the affirmative vote or consent of Members holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the total Percentage Interests.

1.21 "Transfer" means any sale, assignment, transfer, conveyance, pledge, hypothecation, encumbrance, or other disposition, whether voluntary or involuntary, by operation of law or otherwise.

1.22 "Treasury Regulations" means the regulations promulgated under the Code by the United States Treasury Department.

1.23 "Unanimous Vote" means the affirmative vote or consent of all Members.

ARTICLE V - CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND CAPITAL ACCOUNTS

5.1 Initial Capital Contributions. Each Member shall make the initial Capital Contribution set forth opposite such Member's name on Exhibit A.

5.2 Additional Capital Contributions.

  • (a) No Member shall be required to make any additional Capital Contribution to the Company except as may be unanimously agreed by all Members in writing.
  • (b) If the Members determine that additional capital is needed for the Company's business, each Member shall have the opportunity to contribute additional capital in proportion to such Member's Percentage Interest. If any Member does not contribute such Member's proportionate share within thirty (30) days after notice, the other Members may contribute such amount, and the Percentage Interests shall be adjusted accordingly.

5.3 Capital Accounts. A Capital Account shall be established and maintained for each Member in accordance with Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv). Each Member's Capital Account shall be:

  • (a) Increased by: (i) the amount of money contributed by such Member to the Company; (ii) the fair market value of property contributed by such Member to the Company (net of liabilities secured by such contributed property that the Company is considered to assume or take subject to); and (iii) allocations to such Member of Company income and gain (or items thereof), including income and gain exempt from tax and income and gain described in Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv)(g), but excluding income and gain described in Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(4)(i);
  • (b) Decreased by: (i) the amount of money distributed to such Member by the Company; (ii) the fair market value of property distributed to such Member by the Company (net of liabilities secured by such distributed property that such Member is considered to assume or take subject to); (iii) allocations to such Member of expenditures of the Company described in Section 705(a)(2)(B) of the Code; and (iv) allocations to such Member of Company loss and deduction (or items thereof), including loss and deduction described in Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv)(g), but excluding items described in clause (iii) above and loss or deduction described in Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(4)(i) or 1.704-1(b)(4)(iii).

5.4 No Interest on Capital. No interest shall be paid on Capital Contributions or on balances in Capital Accounts.

5.5 No Withdrawal of Capital. No Member shall have the right to withdraw or receive any return of such Member's Capital Contribution except as specifically provided in this Agreement or as may be required by law.

5.6 Loans by Members. Any Member may, with the approval of a Majority Vote, make loans to the Company. Any such loan shall not be considered a Capital Contribution and shall not increase the lending Member's Percentage Interest. The Company shall repay such loans with interest at a rate to be agreed upon by the lending Member and the Company.

ARTICLE VI - ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

6.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses. Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, the Company's net profits and net losses (and each item of Company income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit) for each Fiscal Year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests.

6.2 Regulatory Allocations. Notwithstanding the provisions of Section 6.1:

  • (a) Minimum Gain Chargeback. If there is a net decrease in Company minimum gain during any Fiscal Year, each Member shall be allocated items of Company income and gain for such Fiscal Year (and, if necessary, subsequent Fiscal Years) in an amount equal to such Member's share of the net decrease in Company minimum gain, determined in accordance with Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-2(g).
  • (b) Member Nonrecourse Debt Minimum Gain Chargeback. If there is a net decrease in Member nonrecourse debt minimum gain attributable to a Member nonrecourse debt during any Fiscal Year, each Member who has a share of such Member nonrecourse debt minimum gain, determined in accordance with Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-2(i)(5), shall be allocated items of Company income and gain for such Fiscal Year (and, if necessary, subsequent Fiscal Years) in an amount equal to such Member's share of the net decrease in such Member nonrecourse debt minimum gain.
  • (c) Qualified Income Offset. If any Member unexpectedly receives any adjustments, allocations, or distributions described in Treasury Regulations Sections 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(d)(4), (5), or (6), items of Company income and gain shall be specially allocated to such Member in an amount and manner sufficient to eliminate, to the extent required by the Treasury Regulations, the adjusted capital account deficit of such Member as quickly as possible.
  • (d) Nonrecourse Deductions. Nonrecourse deductions for any Fiscal Year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests.
  • (e) Member Nonrecourse Deductions. Any Member nonrecourse deductions for any Fiscal Year shall be allocated to the Member who bears the economic risk of loss with respect to the Member nonrecourse debt to which such Member nonrecourse deductions are attributable.

6.3 Tax Allocations. For income tax purposes, each item of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit of the Company shall be allocated among the Members in the same manner as its correlative item of "book" income, gain, loss, or deduction is allocated pursuant to this Article VI, except as otherwise required by Code Section 704(c) and the Treasury Regulations thereunder.

6.4 Distributions.

  • (a) Timing and Amount. Distributions of Distributable Cash shall be made to the Members at such times and in such amounts as determined by a Majority Vote of the Members.
  • (b) Proportionate Distributions. Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, all Distributions shall be made to the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests.
  • (c) Tax Distributions. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Company shall distribute to each Member, at least quarterly, an amount sufficient to enable each Member to pay federal and state income taxes on Company income allocated to such Member for such period, calculated at the highest marginal individual income tax rate then in effect for the relevant jurisdiction.

6.5 Limitations on Distributions. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, no Distribution shall be made to any Member if, after giving effect to such Distribution:

  • (a) The Company would be unable to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of business; or
  • (b) The Company's total assets would be less than the sum of its total liabilities plus the amount that would be needed, if the Company were to be dissolved at the time of the Distribution, to satisfy the preferential rights upon dissolution of Members whose preferential rights are superior to those of the Members receiving the Distribution.

7.1 Member-Managed Company. The Company shall be member-managed. Except as otherwise expressly provided in this Agreement, the business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by the Members.

7.2 Authority of Members.

  • (b) Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, any difference arising among Members as to matters connected with the business of the Company shall be decided by a Majority Vote.

  • (i) Commencement or settlement of any litigation or legal proceeding involving potential liability exceeding $50,000;

ARTICLE VIII - TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

8.1 General Restriction on Transfer. No Member may Transfer all or any portion of such Member's Membership Interest without the prior written consent of all other Members, which consent may be withheld in the sole and absolute discretion of any Member.

8.2 Right of First Refusal.

  • (a) Before any Member (the "Transferring Member") may Transfer any Membership Interest to a third party, the Transferring Member must first offer to sell such Membership Interest to the Company and the other Members (the "Remaining Members") at the same price and on the same terms as the proposed Transfer.
  • (b) The Transferring Member shall give written notice (the "Transfer Notice") to the Company and the Remaining Members, specifying the terms of the proposed Transfer and the identity of the proposed transferee.
  • (c) The Company shall have thirty (30) days from receipt of the Transfer Notice to elect to purchase all (but not less than all) of the offered Membership Interest.
  • (d) If the Company does not elect to purchase the offered Membership Interest, the Remaining Members shall have an additional thirty (30) days to elect to purchase the offered Membership Interest, pro rata in accordance with their Percentage Interests (or in such other proportions as they may agree).
  • (e) If neither the Company nor the Remaining Members elect to purchase the offered Membership Interest, the Transferring Member may Transfer such Membership Interest to the proposed transferee at a price no less than that set forth in the Transfer Notice and on terms no more favorable to the transferee than those set forth in the Transfer Notice, provided that such Transfer is completed within ninety (90) days after the expiration of the Remaining Members' election period.

8.3 Permitted Transfers. Notwithstanding Section 8.1, a Member may Transfer all or any portion of such Member's Membership Interest to any of the following without the consent of the other Members:

  • (a) A trust for the benefit of the Member or the Member's immediate family members (spouse, children, grandchildren, parents, or siblings);
  • (b) The Member's spouse or lineal descendants;
  • (c) An entity wholly owned by the Member;
  • (d) Another Member.

8.4 Conditions to All Transfers. Any Transfer of a Membership Interest (whether or not requiring the consent of the other Members) shall be subject to the following conditions:

  • (a) The transferee shall execute a written agreement to be bound by all the terms and provisions of this Agreement;
  • (b) The transferee shall provide such information as the Company may reasonably request;
  • (c) The Transfer shall not violate any applicable federal or state securities laws;
  • (d) The transferee shall pay all costs and expenses incurred by the Company in connection with the Transfer;
  • (e) The Transfer shall not cause the Company to be treated as a "publicly traded partnership" within the meaning of Section 7704 of the Code.

8.5 Effect of Transfer.

  • (a) A transferee of a Membership Interest who is not admitted as a Member shall be entitled only to receive the share of profits, losses, and Distributions to which the transferor would otherwise be entitled. Such transferee shall not be entitled to participate in the management of the Company or to exercise any rights of a Member.
  • (b) A transferee of a Membership Interest shall be admitted as a Member only upon satisfaction of the conditions set forth in Article IV.

8.6 Involuntary Transfers. If a creditor of a Member obtains a charging order against the Member's Membership Interest, the Company shall have the option to purchase such Membership Interest at fair market value, as determined in accordance with Section 8.7.

8.7 Valuation. For purposes of this Article VIII, the fair market value of a Membership Interest shall be determined as follows:

  • (a) The parties shall first attempt to agree upon fair market value within thirty (30) days.
  • (b) If they are unable to agree, the fair market value shall be determined by an independent appraiser mutually selected by the parties (or, if they cannot agree on an appraiser, by an appraiser selected by the American Arbitration Association).
  • (c) The appraiser's determination shall be final and binding on all parties, and the costs of the appraisal shall be borne equally by the parties.

ARTICLE IX - WITHDRAWAL; DISSOLUTION

9.1 Voluntary Withdrawal.

  • (a) A Member may not withdraw from the Company prior to the dissolution and winding up of the Company except with the unanimous consent of all other Members.
  • (b) A Member who wishes to withdraw must provide at least ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other Members.
  • (c) Upon a permitted withdrawal, the withdrawing Member shall be entitled to receive the fair market value of such Member's Membership Interest, determined as of the date of withdrawal in accordance with Section 8.7.

9.2 Events of Dissociation. A Member shall be dissociated from the Company upon the occurrence of any of the following events:

  • (a) The Member's voluntary withdrawal with the consent of all other Members;
  • (b) The Member's death or incapacity;
  • (c) The Member becoming a debtor in a bankruptcy case;
  • (d) The Member's expulsion by the unanimous vote of all other Members upon a determination that the Member has materially breached this Agreement and failed to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after written notice;
  • (e) Any other event specified in the Act as causing dissociation.

9.3 Effect of Dissociation. Upon dissociation, a dissociated Member (or the Member's estate, representative, or successor) shall have only the rights of a transferee of a Membership Interest, unless the dissociated Member is admitted as a Member in accordance with Article IV.

9.4 Purchase of Dissociated Member's Interest. Upon dissociation of a Member, the Company or the Remaining Members shall have the option, but not the obligation, to purchase all (but not less than all) of the dissociated Member's Membership Interest at fair market value, determined as of the date of dissociation in accordance with Section 8.7.

9.5 Dissolution. The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events:

  • (a) The unanimous written consent of all Members;
  • (b) The sale or other disposition of all or substantially all of the Company's assets;
  • (c) The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act;
  • (d) Administrative dissolution by the South Carolina Secretary of State;
  • (e) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company's business to be continued;
  • (f) Any other event specified in the Articles of Organization as causing dissolution.

9.6 Winding Up.

  • (a) Upon dissolution, the Members (or a liquidating trustee appointed by the Members) shall wind up the Company's affairs and liquidate its assets.
  • (b) The Company's assets shall be applied and distributed in the following order of priority:
    1. First, to the payment of debts and liabilities of the Company to creditors, including Members who are creditors, to the extent permitted by law;
    2. Second, to the payment of expenses of liquidation;
    3. Third, to the establishment of any reserves that the Members deem reasonably necessary for contingent or unforeseen liabilities;
    4. Fourth, to the Members in proportion to their positive Capital Account balances, after taking into account all Capital Account adjustments for the Fiscal Year during which the liquidation occurs.

9.7 Articles of Termination. Upon completion of the winding up of the Company's affairs, the Members shall cause articles of termination to be filed with the South Carolina Secretary of State.

ARTICLE X - INDEMNIFICATION

10.1 Indemnification. To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Company shall indemnify and hold harmless each Member, Manager (if any), officer, employee, and agent of the Company (each, an "Indemnified Person") from and against any and all losses, claims, damages, liabilities, expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and expenses), judgments, fines, settlements, and other amounts arising from any and all claims, demands, actions, suits, or proceedings, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative, in which the Indemnified Person may be involved, or threatened to be involved, as a party or otherwise, by reason of the Indemnified Person's status as a Member, Manager, officer, employee, or agent of the Company or the Indemnified Person's service at the request of the Company as a director, officer, manager, member, partner, employee, or agent of any other entity; provided, however, that no Indemnified Person shall be indemnified for any losses, claims, damages, liabilities, expenses, judgments, fines, settlements, or other amounts arising from such Indemnified Person's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or knowing violation of law.

10.2 Advancement of Expenses. The Company shall advance to any Indemnified Person reasonable attorneys' fees and other costs and expenses incurred in connection with the defense of any action, suit, or proceeding in advance of the final disposition of such action, suit, or proceeding, upon receipt of an undertaking by or on behalf of the Indemnified Person to repay such amount if it is ultimately determined that such Indemnified Person is not entitled to be indemnified as provided in Section 10.1.

10.3 Insurance. The Company may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any Indemnified Person against any liability asserted against such Indemnified Person and incurred by such Indemnified Person in any capacity described in Section 10.1, whether or not the Company would have the power to indemnify such Indemnified Person against such liability under the provisions of this Article X.

10.4 Non-Exclusive. The rights to indemnification and advancement of expenses provided in this Article X shall not be exclusive of any other rights to which any Indemnified Person may be entitled under any agreement, vote of Members, provision of law, or otherwise.

ARTICLE XIII - CONFIDENTIALITY

13.1 Confidential Information. Each Member agrees to maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information and not to disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company, except as required by law or to such Member's advisors who agree to maintain confidentiality.

13.2 Survival. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Article XIII shall survive any termination of a Member's Membership Interest and shall continue indefinitely.

ARTICLE XIV - MISCELLANEOUS

14.1 Governing Law. This Agreement and the rights and obligations of the Members hereunder shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of South Carolina, without regard to principles of conflicts of law.

14.2 Dispute Resolution.

  • (a) Mediation. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation in Charleston, South Carolina, in accordance with the Commercial Mediation Procedures of the American Arbitration Association.
