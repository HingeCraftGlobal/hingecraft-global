1. DEFINITIONS

"Applicable Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws relating to privacy, data protection, and data security, including the GDPR, CCPA, and other relevant laws.

"Controller" means the natural or legal person that determines the purposes and means of Processing Personal Data.

"Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

"GDPR" means the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679.

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

"Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

"Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, and deletion.

"Processor" means a natural or legal person that Processes Personal Data on behalf of a Controller.

"Services" means the services provided by Processor to Controller under the underlying agreement.

"Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE

2.1 Scope. This DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services.

2.2 Processing Details. The details of Processing are set forth in Annex 1, including:

  • (a) Subject matter and duration of Processing;
  • (b) Nature and purpose of Processing;
  • (c) Types of Personal Data;
  • (d) Categories of Data Subjects.

2.3 Relationship. Controller is the Controller and Processor is the Processor with respect to Personal Data Processed under this DPA. Nothing in this DPA shall be construed to make Processor a joint Controller.

3. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

Controller represents, warrants, and agrees that:

  • (a) It has complied with all Applicable Data Protection Laws in collecting Personal Data;
  • (b) It has provided all required notices and obtained all necessary consents;
  • (c) It has a lawful basis for Processing under Applicable Data Protection Laws;
  • (d) Its Processing instructions to Processor are lawful;
  • (e) It will respond to Data Subject requests in accordance with Applicable Data Protection Laws;
  • (f) It will maintain appropriate security measures for Personal Data in its possession.

4. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

4.1 Processing Instructions

Processor shall:

  • (a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller;
  • (b) Inform Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Laws;
  • (c) Not Process Personal Data for any purpose other than providing the Services;
  • (d) Not sell, share (as defined by CCPA), or use Personal Data for its own purposes.

4.2 Confidentiality

Processor shall:

  • (a) Ensure that persons authorized to Process Personal Data are subject to confidentiality obligations;
  • (b) Limit access to Personal Data to personnel who need access to perform the Services;
  • (c) Treat Personal Data as confidential information.

4.3 Security Measures

Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

  • (a) Encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest;
  • (b) Access controls and authentication mechanisms;
  • (c) Regular testing and evaluation of security measures;
  • (d) Ability to restore availability of Personal Data;
  • (e) Physical security of facilities;
  • (f) Employee security training.

Specific security measures are described in Annex 2.

4.4 Sub-processors

Processor shall:

  • (a) Not engage Sub-processors without Controller's prior [written consent / general authorization];
  • (b) Maintain a list of authorized Sub-processors (Annex 3);
  • (c) Notify Controller of intended changes to Sub-processors and allow Controller to object;
  • (d) Ensure Sub-processors are bound by data protection obligations no less protective than this DPA;
  • (e) Remain liable for Sub-processors' acts and omissions.

4.5 Data Subject Rights

Processor shall:

  • (a) Assist Controller in responding to Data Subject requests;
  • (b) Notify Controller promptly of any Data Subject requests received directly;
  • (c) Not respond directly to Data Subject requests unless authorized by Controller;
  • (d) Implement technical measures to facilitate Data Subject rights (access, correction, deletion, portability).

4.6 Personal Data Breaches

Processor shall:

  • (a) Notify Controller without undue delay (and no later than 48 hours) upon becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach;
  • (b) Provide information necessary for Controller to comply with breach notification obligations;
  • (c) Cooperate with Controller in investigating and mitigating the breach;
  • (d) Document Personal Data Breaches and remedial actions.

4.7 Data Protection Impact Assessments

Processor shall provide reasonable assistance to Controller with data protection impact assessments and consultations with supervisory authorities where required.

4.8 Compliance Demonstration

Processor shall:

  • (a) Make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance;
  • (b) Allow for and contribute to audits conducted by Controller or an auditor;
  • (c) Provide audit reports, certifications, or other evidence of compliance upon request.

5. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

5.1 Transfers. Personal Data may be transferred to and Processed in countries outside the EEA/UK, including the United States, where Processor or its Sub-processors maintain facilities.

5.2 Transfer Mechanisms. For transfers from the EEA/UK to countries without an adequacy decision, the parties shall rely on:

  • (a) Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted by the European Commission;
  • (b) Binding Corporate Rules (where applicable);
  • (c) Other approved transfer mechanisms under GDPR Article 46.

5.3 SCCs. The Standard Contractual Clauses set forth in Annex 4 are incorporated by reference and form part of this DPA.

5.4 Supplementary Measures. Processor shall implement supplementary measures as necessary to ensure an essentially equivalent level of protection for transferred data.

6. RETURN AND DELETION

6.1 Upon Termination. Upon termination or expiration of the Services, Processor shall, at Controller's election:

  • (a) Return all Personal Data to Controller; and/or
  • (b) Delete all Personal Data, unless retention is required by law.

6.2 Certification. Upon request, Processor shall certify in writing that it has complied with deletion obligations.

6.3 Retention Period. Controller must make its election within 30 days of termination. If no election is made, Processor may delete Personal Data.

7. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Liability. Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability in the underlying Services agreement.

7.2 Indemnification. Each party shall indemnify the other for losses arising from the indemnifying party's breach of this DPA or Applicable Data Protection Laws.

8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 Term. This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of Processing under the Services agreement.

8.2 Survival. Sections that by their nature should survive termination shall survive, including confidentiality, return/deletion, and liability provisions.

9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1 Governing Law. This DPA shall be governed by the laws of the State of South Carolina, except that the SCCs shall be governed as set forth therein.

9.2 Conflicts. In the event of conflict between this DPA and the underlying agreement, this DPA shall prevail with respect to data protection matters.

9.3 Amendment. This DPA may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.

9.4 Severability. If any provision is found invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect.
